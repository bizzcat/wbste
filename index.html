Training & roots

Why I’m doing this, and what has shaped the way I work.

For the last six years, I’ve been full-time devoted to training in spiritual, therapeutic, and relational practices.

Four of those years were spent living in multiple Buddhist monasteries — a year and a half of which on silent meditation retreat, under the steady guidance of skilled teachers.

Another central practice has been Circling, which cultivates trust and intimacy in every relationship, helps us navigate charged moments with steadiness, and invites a way of living that is more truthful, courageous, and natural.

I consider myself supremely lucky. Still, it’s not just luck that afforded me this depth of training. Ingenuity, effort, and yes, unearned privilege all played a part — but the single greatest enabling factor has been generosity.

I’ve received more than I can ever recount from teachers, monasteries, friends, foundations, and a loving, loyal family. As I conclude this season of intensive training, I’m committed to keeping generosity at the basis of my livelihood.

This coaching practice is one way of paying that forward: sharing everything I’ve learned with anyone who feels they might benefit.

What a session looks like

A session is one hour where my attention is fully with you. Within that space, either:

  • You set the agenda,
  • we figure it out together, or
  • I take the lead based on what I sense would serve

We might curate a meditation curriculum based on your goals, with regular check-ins and guided meditations to keep momentum.

I can witness you without judgment as you navigate emotions and speak to life’s challenges, offering gentle reflections, reframes, nudges, and — only rarely and with consent — advice.

I’ve discovered that play is often half the medicine. So if what’s needed is to bop around our rooms and babble like banshees, or launch into a howling recital of Bohemian Rhapsody … I’m here for it.

We can explore such themes as: embodiment, boundaries, control vs. surrender, masculine - feminine dynamics, making wise commitments, giving and receiving feedback skillfully, reclaiming anger and wielding it cleanly, how to navigate manipulative relationships, nervous system regulation, and using the breath to cultivate the mind.

Donation-based pricing

Generosity at the center — not as an afterthought.

While the market suggests I could charge a few hundred dollars per session, I’m offering them instead by donation to anyone who feels they might benefit.

This means that after our session you could send me a single dollar and I would rejoice. Truly — you could send me $500 and I wouldn’t hold a single grudge against you.

Whatever you offer enables me to live a simple life and:

  • Freely coach others,
  • Write with more regularity,
  • Support altruistic projects,
  • Show up more fully for my troubled country, and
  • Dance on the weekend with friends.

If donation-based is uncomfortable, we can talk through that — sometimes money brings up a lot of material, and that’s welcome here.

Or we can simply set a fixed fee together that feels clear and workable for both of us.

My hope is that this structure allows people with less access to resources to still receive support, while those with more can help sustain the work for others.
